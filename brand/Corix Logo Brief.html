The Minimal C

A C drawn with three squares, and the negative space between them.

The Corix mark is built on a 3×3 grid. Three cells are filled — top-center, mid-left, bottom-center. The eye completes the C from what isn't there. This brief covers how to use it.

01 / Construction

The mark

Three rounded squares on a 3×3 grid. Each cell occupies 23% of the icon width; gaps are 5.5%. Corner radius is 17% of the cell size. The grid is never drawn — only implied.

03 / Clear space

Breathing room

Always preserve clear space equal to one cell ("x") around the mark in every direction. Nothing else — text, edges, other logos — may enter this zone.

x = The height of one cell in the mark
Apply to All four sides of the mark, and to lockups (measured from the wordmark edge).
Inside boundaries No type, no other logos, no graphic elements, no edges.

05 / Color

Light and dark

The mark exists in black or white only. Choose based on background, not preference. Black on light is primary; white on dark is the inverse. There is no greyscale, no gradient, no tint.

06 / In context

Application

How the mark behaves across surfaces. The lockup is preferred in long-form contexts (web headers, documents, signatures). The mark stands alone where space is square or scarce (avatars, favicons, app icons).

Note · Optical balance

Center it optically, not mathematically.

The mark's mid-row cell anchors the left side, so its visual mass sits left of geometric center. When placed inside a circle or square, nudge the mark 12% rightward of the container's true center. Don't redraw the mark — the asymmetry is intentional. Only the placement changes.

07 / Do & Don't

Misuse

A short list. The mark is uncomplicated; its integrity comes from restraint.

Do

Use Ink on Paper. The default. Always preferred.

Do

Inverse on dark surfaces. Mark becomes Paper, never grey.

Do

Tinted backgrounds are fine if contrast ≥ 4.5:1 against the mark.

Don't

No gradients, no color fills. The mark is monochrome — Ink or Paper only.

Don't

Never stretch, skew, or alter the proportions. Scale uniformly.

Don't

Don't add, move, or remove cells. The pattern is the identity.
